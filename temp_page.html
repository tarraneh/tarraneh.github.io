Tarraneh Eftekhari

Ph.D. Student, Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics

I am a second year graduate student at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. My research focuses on transient phenomena and radio instrumentation. I am currently working with my advisor, Edo Berger, on multi-wavelength followup observations of the jetted tidal disruption event, Sw1644+57. I am also working on the design and development of a low noise amplifier for the Large Aperture Experiment to Detect the Dark Ages.


You can find my blog at balderandtyr.com.
