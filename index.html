Building a Hackintosh

A lean, mean Hackintosh-ing machine

Hackintosh computers have been online for nearly a decade now (since the switch to Intel processors), allowing users to implement Mac OS X on non-Apple branded hardware, achieving performance close to or comparable to the latest Mac Pros for a fraction of the price. In addition to installing Mac OS X on a custom-configured computer -- optimized for your needs -- dual-booting Windows on a Hackintosh allows for your computer to moonlight as a high-end gaming machine (depending on your hardware, of course). But the road to a fully-functioning stable Hackintosh is unsurprisingly not a walk in the park. It requires a willingness to do some research in order to determine compatible hardware, spend some time aquiring the appropriate kexts, and most importantly, remaining on your gaurd even post-installation for any hiccups down the road (which will inevitably arise). The following post is meant to help you navigate your way to Hackintosh glory. MacBreaker
