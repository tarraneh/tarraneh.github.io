Tarraneh Eftekhari

I am a second year graduate student at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics. My research focuses on transient phenomena and radio instrumentation. In particular, I work on multi-wavelength observations of tidal disruption events which allow for the characterization of the extreme environments surrounding black holes. In the lab, I work on components for low frequency radio arrays in an attempt to detect neutral Hydrogen from the very early universe.

In the past, I've worked as part of the Long Wavelength Array collaboration based out of the University of New Mexico. As part of my work there, I conducted a survey of giant pulses from the Crab Pulsar, characterizing the statistical distributions of these high intensity bursts between 10 - 100 MHz.

Though I'm east-coast bound for the time being, I'm a desert dweller at heart, hailing from the enchanted mountains of Northern New Mexico.
